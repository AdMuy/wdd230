PARC: Contrast

Illo Studio
https://illo.tv/studio

I choose this page as it demonstrates good use of color and contrast. While it's a simple idea at its core, showing the people working in this Studio and what are their responsibilities, the execution with each of them having a photo that matches the background color it's perfect.
I think it directs your attention and makes you want to see all of the pictures on the website and most importantly create an instant connection with the people who work in this studio. This is accomplished by the vibrant colors against a white background and the great contrast that exists between them.

Fitt's Law

The McBride Company
https://mcbridedesign.com/

For me this is one of the best examples of the Fitt's principle, the website is for a company whose work ranges from Graphic Desing to Interior Desing passing through Architectural Desing, and they have worked with big brands.
They could have included all this information on their main page, however they opted for 3 main options that encampulates their field of work.
The three options are centered with the same font size, I think this is perfect to guide the user to one of three posibilites and then expand the options based on their initial desire.

Hick's Law

PayPal
http://paypal.com/

I liked this site to exemplify Hick's Law, as soon as we open the page we are presented with two options at the top, the Menu and Log In options. Then in the middle two arrows to know more about purchasing or selling online.
Most likely the users comming to this page will want to access their account and make an online transaction. While new commers to the platform can check the Menu options or the arrows and see all the posibilites the platform can present and know more about the service given by this company.
